the story of frankenstein

book design

( 2 )

The Story of Frankenstein is a visual re-imagining of the famous novel by Mary Shelley. Through the metaphoric use of the kaleidoscope, it explores the relation of the individual to the other, against the back-drop of the romantic-individualism echoed in the novel, and offers a critique of this relation through the use of Karpman’s Drama Triangle—a social model of human interaction that maps a type of destructive behaviour between people in conflict. A tool used in psychotherapy, which is here used to analyse the interaction between the characters and its consequences.

The books visually convey the workings of the Drama Triangle by showing, in each edition, only one side of either one of the three narrator’s stories, showing the text where there’s a monologue and mirroring the lines spoken bij another character whenever they’re in a dialogue, stressing the unwillingness of all characters to consider each—others point-of-view, which lies at the heart of their destructive behaviour. It pushes them back more into their own cognitive world, and creates a bigger space between them.

This design makes it essential to have all the books together to be able to follow the whole story, seeing it from all sides and so shows how we can’t make full sense of ourselves, our own behaviour in a situation, without it being reflected by others.

ABOUT

Robin de haan is a graphic designer based in Rotterdam, The Netherlands, specialised in experimental book / publication design and web-development. With an interest in understanding (art) history to try to grasp the present, her book designs make use of metaphors to explore different facets of our human nature and current social landscape. Reflections, patterns and narrative devices have been recurring elements, whereas bold color contrasts, nostalgic photography and a sensibility to typography define her work.
